Stanford OpenLab

We are a frontier research lab housed at Stanford University. As a rule, we reject academic norms in favor of nontraditional approaches, leading to outputs that would be impossible to produce anywhere else.

Our mission is simple: make moonshots a reality.

About OpenLab

We are a team of researchers, tinkerers, and creators who are passionate about solving the pressing problems facing humanity today. While academia is usually organized by departments and disciplines, we intentionally seek out and house researchers from fields that normally don't go together and may even be at odds. By putting brilliant minds in such a novel environment—surrounding them not with people who think as they do and approach problems in similar ways, but with others who have completely different perspectives, tools, and preconceptions—we draw out unexpected connections.

OpenLab is built around a culture of creativity and experimentation. Our researchers are experts in their respective fields, but see the value in stepping outside of their intellectual comfort zone to work closely with peers who think in fundamentally different ways. The result is a space optimized for the cross-pollination of diverse ideas, allowing room for new and surprising insights.

OpenLab Research

OpenLab research is organized around macro research pillars, with independent research groups or smaller projects/initiatives nested under each pillar. Our current research pillars are:

Society and Algorithms

Examining societal structures through a technological lens, with a particular focus on AI, coordination mechanisms, and building safe systems. Our two research groups focus on social choice theory, as well as AI safety & governance.

Human Prototyping

Building the unglamorous, low-profit prototypes that improve human existence. At the moment, we are actively working in improving access to the NIH’s All of Us database, as well as building tools for individuals with chronic conditions.

Decentralized Systems

Prototyping new technologies to fight against a dystopian future, focusing on privacy, governance, blockchains, and other resilient infrastructure. Research groups under this pillar focus on DAO governance and applied blockchains.

Arts & Letters

Exploring the future of art and philosophy by applying the humanities to questions typically assumed to have “hard science” answers—and vice versa. We offer a theology research fellowship and a creative writing residency.

OpenLab Initiatives

OpenLab does more than research. Occasionally, we notice a missing piece of infratructure that we believe would help incentivize "good" or interesting human progress; something that should exist, but doesn't. So we build it. A few of our initiatives include:

OpenLab Conference

In stark contrast to events that center around a particular discipline, we assemble an agenda around interesting ideas from any field. Participants are uniquely exposed to novel feedback, ideas, and perspectives.

Failure Award

Not all failures are bad—some are a necessary step for progress, and these ought to be celebrated. To recognize the pioneers who took a risk, we offer a prize to one researcher a year who fails for the right reasons.

Early Career Research Grant

Young researchers often need to follow the money rather than their curiosity. The best work, however, is driven by passion, so we fund promising early-career reseachers as they explore their (academic) passions for a year.

Retroactive Funding Prize

A lot of research or other open-source work is difficult to fund until it's successful—but at that point, why fund it? To fix this dilemma, we offer a retroactive funding prize for successful, impactful public work.

Join OpenLab

There are several ways to get involved with OpenLab, whether you're an academic, an industry stakeholder, or a member of the public.

Research Meetings

We host weekly research meetings that cover a wide range of topics, designed to offer exposure to niche corners of the world. The only requirement is that the work be interesting—think of these as little academic TED talks!

Research Collaborations

OpenLab works with external stakholders to advance humanity. We take on sponsored research, team up with experts in industry, government, & academia on research projects, and house important ad-hoc research efforts.

Support OpenLab

OpenLab exists thanks to our Consortium members, grant-giving organization, and other donors. We offer several levels of membership, including customized packages. Please reach out to discuss supporting our work!
